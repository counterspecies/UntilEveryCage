About Until Every Cage is Empty

What Is This Project?

Until Every Cage is Empty is an interactive map that exposes the vast infrastructure of animal exploitation across the world. Using publicly available government data, we've compiled and mapped tens of thousands of facilities including slaughterhouses, processing plants, animal research laboratories, commercial breeders, dealers, and exhibitors.

This comprehensive mapping tool brings together data from multiple federal agencies (USDA in the U.S., BVL in Germany, FSA in the UK) into a single, searchable interface that allows users to discover the reality of animal exploitation operating in their own communities and across the nation.

Our Motivation

We created this tool because transparency drives change. The animal exploitation industry deliberately operates out of public view, with facilities strategically hidden in rural areas and industrial zones. By making this concealed infrastructure visible and accessible, we aim to:

  • Shatter Ignorance: Help people confront the true scale and geographic reach of animal exploitation in America
  • Fuel Investigation: Provide researchers, journalists, and advocates with the data they need to expose wrongdoing and demand accountability
  • Power Resistance: Give animal rights activists precise information to organize targeted campaigns and disrupt business as usual
  • Force Accountability: Make it impossible for facilities to hide violations and welfare abuses from public scrutiny

Why This Matters

Every year, billions of animals are processed through the facilities mapped on this site. These are sentient beings with the capacity for pain, fear, and suffering, yet their exploitation happens behind walls designed to keep you from witnessing it. By mapping where this violence occurs, we believe we can:

  • Strip away the industry's veil of secrecy and force public reckoning
  • Arm consumers and policymakers with the uncomfortable truths they need
  • Connect local communities with the hidden exploitation operating in their backyards
  • Challenge an industry that profits from public ignorance

Our Commitment

This project is committed to accuracy, transparency, and relentless accountability. We use only publicly available government data and encourage users to approach their advocacy work through legal, strategic channels. Our goal is not simply to document the problem, but to provide the tools necessary to dismantle it.
