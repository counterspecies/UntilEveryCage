Until Every Cage is Empty

Let's be brutally clear: behind the reinforced concrete and locked gates, an ongoing atrocity is unfolding. This is not some distant threat; it is a full-scale war against the innocent, happening right now. An industrial machine of death is operating without pause, fueled by your complacency.

This machine is vast, but its workings are simple: confinement, torture, and systematic slaughter. Every single year, over 9 billion chickens are shackled upside down, their throats slit by automated blades, with many boiled alive in scalding feather-removal tanks. Alongside them, 120 million pigs are herded into gas chambers, screaming as CO2 burns their lungs from the inside out, while 30 million cows have a steel bolt shot through their skull before being hoisted by a leg to bleed out, often while still conscious. This is not an accident; it is the intended outcome.

The individuals trapped within this system are not commodities. They are sentient beings with an undeniable will to live, a profound capacity for fear, and an inherent right to freedom. To treat them as mere units of production is not just unethicalâ€”it is a profound moral failure that stains our collective conscience.

Forget your calls for kinder cruelty. There is no such thing. To advocate for bigger cages or "humane" slaughter is to tinker at the edges of a fundamentally evil enterprise. We are not seeking to reform injustice; we are here to dismantle it entirely. You cannot negotiate ethics with those who profit from pain.

There is no conceivable justification for this ongoing tyranny. Every single life taken within this system is a moral outrage. The only acceptable response, the only ethical imperative, is the complete and immediate liberation of all those held captive.

The clock is ticking for billions. Your first act is mandatory: You must go vegan. Now. But that is just the starting point. You must immediately become a direct force of disruption against this system. Organize, confront, and dismantle the machinery of suffering. Silence is complicity. Inaction is betrayal.

To those who control the slaughterhouses, the farms, and the laboratories, your days of operating in the shadows are over. We see your facilities. We know your names. We understand your methods. Your profits are built on unimaginable pain, and that foundation is cracking. The price for your cruelty will be paid. Justice is coming, and it will be swift and absolute. Empty cages are not a request; they are an inevitability.

Our goal is not reform. Our goal is the utter destruction of this barbaric industry. We will not rest. We will not falter. Until every cage is empty.
